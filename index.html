WHAT IS BUILT

This platform is a full digital replica of a two-sheet Excel ROI model. Every formula from the Excel has been converted into live JavaScript — so the moment you touch any slider or toggle, all 13 calculations update simultaneously with zero page reload.

Inputs Sheet Replicated as

The left panel. Contains all raw user inputs (retailers, fee, months, days, maintenance, farmers/day, ticket size, purchases/farmer, conversion rates) plus the 9 editable setup cost line items. Every derived value shown below the sliders mirrors the Inputs sheet's formula section exactly.

ROI Model Sheet Replicated as

The right panel. Contains the 4 investment summary cards, the intermediate calculation grid, the 3 scenario cards (Min / Median / Max), and the 4 comparison bar charts. All values here are computed live from the inputs — exactly as Excel's cross-sheet references work.

HOW THE 2 SHEETS CONNECT

In Excel, the ROI Model sheet pulls values from Inputs via direct cell references like =Inputs!B37. In this platform, the same dependency is replicated in JavaScript — the right panel always reads from whatever the left panel's sliders currently say.

THE 13 FORMULAS

Every formula below runs live in your browser. The formula shown is the exact Excel formula — mapped 1:1 to JavaScript.

Inputs Sheet Formulas (5)
=ROUND(B6×B7, 0)
Monthly retailer licensing — Retailers mapped × Fee per month. Base building block for all licensing costs.
=ROUND(B20×B8, 0)
Licensing total — Monthly licensing × Campaign duration (months). Total you pay for all retailers over the full term.
=ROUND(B9×B8, 0)
Maintenance total — Monthly maintenance cost × Months. Platform upkeep over campaign duration.
=ROUND(B8×B11, 0)
Campaign days — Months × Operating days per month. How many total days the campaign runs. This was missing before — now added.
=ROUND(B6×B10×B23, 0)
Total farmers engaged — Retailers × Farmers per retailer per day × Campaign days. Total touchpoints (not unique).
ROI Model Sheet Formulas — per scenario (×3)
=ROUND(Farmers×CR, 0)
Converted farmers — Total farmers engaged × Conversion rate (8/10/12%). The only thing that differs across the 3 scenarios.
=ROUND(Conv×Purch, 0)
Verified purchases — Converted farmers × Purchases per converted farmer. Models repeat-purchase behaviour when >1.
=ROUND(Verif×Ticket, 0)
Revenue generated — Verified purchases × Average ticket size. Top-line revenue attributable to the platform.
=ROUND(TotInv÷Verif, 1)
Cost per verified purchase — Total investment ÷ Verified purchases. How much it costs to drive one verified purchase.
=ROUND(Rev÷TotInv, 1)
ROI multiple — Revenue ÷ Total investment. How many rupees of revenue each rupee invested generates.
=ROUND(Rev−TotInv, 0)
Net revenue after investment — Revenue minus total investment. Absolute profit attributable to the platform.
HOW TOGGLES WORK

There are 3 types of toggles, each with different behaviour:

Input Toggles (sliders)

Toggling OFF locks the slider (greys it out, disables interaction) but keeps its current value in all calculations. Think of it as "freeze this input" — you're saying "don't let this be accidentally moved." The number still flows through every formula.

Setup Cost Toggles

Toggling a setup cost OFF removes it entirely from the investment total. This is the "what if we skip this component?" analysis. The total investment drops, which improves ROI multiples but represents a less complete build.

Scenario Toggles (Min/Med/Max)

Toggling a scenario OFF hides that scenario card and removes it from bar chart comparisons. Useful when presenting — show only the scenarios most relevant to your audience. The bar widths rescale automatically to the remaining active scenarios.

What Happens in Real Time

Every single change — slider move, number edit, toggle flip — triggers one recalculation pass through all 13 formulas in under 1ms. The live chain display at the top of outputs updates too, showing you the actual numbers flowing through each step.
